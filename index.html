In the 2026 elections, key battleground states like Pennsylvania, Arizona, and Georgia will play a pivotal role in determining the balance of power in Congress.

Senate committee assignments are determined by party leadership after each election. Majority party members chair all committees, giving them significant control over which legislation advances to a floor vote.

Additionally, voter turnout in states such as Michigan and Wisconsin is expected to significantly influence Senate races, while demographic shifts in Texas and Florida could impact House seat distributions.
